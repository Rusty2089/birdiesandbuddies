Rules and Scoring
Rules:

  1. You get 2 tee shots off the 1st tee. No mulligans the rest of the round.
  2. All out of bounds will be played as a lateral hazard. That means you get to drop a ball lateral to where your ball crossed the hazard taking a 1 stroke penalty.
  3. Putts inside the length of the putter are considered “gimmies”, but must be conceded by the group.
  4. All putts for birdie or better must be putted out.
  5. To qualify for the closest to the pin contest, your ball must be on the green.
  6. To qualify for the longest drive, your ball must be in the fairway.
  7. To qualify for the sexiest player in tourney, you must have red hair.
Scoring:

All scoring is individual. There are random teams that are drawn, but don't tell someone you are on their team, because frankly, you suck and it would ruin their weekend.

Scoring is based on a points system where you can earn points for each hole based on your score versus par. In this case, high scores are good. Points for each hole are earned as follows:

  • Bogey = 1 point
  • Par = 2 points
  • Birdie = 4 points
  • Eagle = 8 points
  • Double Eagle = 10 points

Your score for each round is the total points you score for that round minus your quota (handicap) for that round. For example, if you start the round with a quota of 10 and score 12 points, you are +2 for that round and those 2 points are added to your overall tournament score (that’s a good thing!).

Your quota for the next round is adjusted by averaging the previous round’s score with the previous round’s quota. In the example above where someone starts with a quota and of 10 and scores 12 points, the quota for the next round is 11. If the average ends in 0.5, the quota is rounded up.

New as of 2019, if a quota adjust to a 25 or more, we are going to cap an adjustment up by 2 points. So if you start with a 26 and shoot a 32, your next day's quota will be 28 instaed of 29. Yeah, this applies to maybe two people... and you still get screwed because you're actually good at golfing.
